Welcome to the Broccoli Page!

Learn about different kinds of broccoli:

Calabrese Broccoli

Calabrese broccoli, also known as standard broccoli, is the most common variety. It has a large, central head of tightly packed, dark green florets and thick, sturdy stalks. Its flavor is slightly bitter and earthy with a nutty undertone. This versatile vegetable is perfect for roasting, steaming, stir-frying, soups, and salads. It's readily available year-round and packed with vitamins and minerals.

Broccolini

Broccolini is a hybrid of broccoli and Chinese broccoli with slender, tender stalks and smaller, floret-like buds at the top. Its taste is milder and sweeter than regular broccoli, with a slightly asparagus-like flavor. It cooks quickly and requires no floret removal, making it ideal for roasting, stir-frying, grilling, and salads. Broccolini is a good source of vitamin C and adds a delicate flavor to various dishes.

Purple Sprouting Broccoli

Purple sprouting broccoli stands out with its unique appearance. It features slender stalks and purple florets that turn green when cooked. Unlike other broccoli types, it produces flavorful side shoots after the main head is harvested. The taste is sweet and nutty, with a less bitter flavor than regular broccoli. This variety is suitable for roasting, steaming, stir-frying, and salads. It's an excellent source of antioxidants and boasts a longer harvest season than other types.

Chinese Broccoli (Gai Lan)

Chinese broccoli, also known as Gai Lan, features flat, broad florets and thick, leafy stalks. Its flavor is strong and slightly bitter with a hint of sweetness. This vegetable is a staple in Asian cuisine and commonly used in stir-frying, steaming, and soups. It's a good source of calcium and vitamin A, adding a unique flavor profile to your dishes.
